# Green Loop Control Tower - 8-10 Minute Demo Script

## 1) Hook (Minute 0-1)

Hong Kong has many recycling points, but textile access is still uneven:
- 106 public-housing estates are >500m from textile recycling.
- 45.6% of public-housing residents are in the >500m burden zone.

Most teams present one fixed plan. We present a control tower that compares policy options under budget and equity priorities.

## 2) Baseline View (Minute 1-2)

Show top KPI cards:
- Estates >500m
- Textile burden
- Diversion range
- District inequality

Explain measured vs modeled:
- Baseline and static-hubs are measured from existing pipeline outputs.
- Mobile-first and hybrid are modeled estimates with explicit uncertainty.

## 3) Scenario Tradeoff (Minute 2-5)

Open **Overview** tab:
- Show policy frontier chart (burden vs diversion vs cost).
- Switch Auto/Manual controls.
- Move budget and equity sliders and explain recommendation changes.

Call out one tradeoff clearly:
- Lower cost does not always mean lower burden.
- Better equity can come with different cost/impact mixes.

## 4) HK Map (Minute 5-7)

Open **Hong Kong Map** tab:
- Show severity coloring for estates.
- Show proposed hubs when scenario includes fixed hubs.
- Use Estate Spotlight selector and describe one high-burden estate.
- Show Top Beneficiary Estates table.

## 5) Equity + Validation (Minute 7-9)

Open **Equity** tab:
- Primary fairness metric: textile burden >500m.
- Secondary fairness metric: district inequality (Gini).
- Explain who benefits (newly served estates, residents moved out of >500m zone).

Open **Assumptions & Validation** tab:
- Show uncertainty ranges and payback ranges.
- Show 90-day pilot sequence:
  1. Baseline measurement
  2. Pilot deployment
  3. Validation and recalibration
  4. Scale or pivot decision

## 6) Close (Minute 9-10)

Recommended takeaway:
- Use the dashboard as a decision tool, not a one-off report.
- Start with pilot deployment, collect measured uplift, and iterate with evidence.

## Q&A Ready Lines

- "Are these exact predictions?"
  - No. Baseline/static are measured; mobile/hybrid are modeled and explicitly labeled.
- "Why this is innovative?"
  - It turns static placement into a policy operating system with transparent tradeoffs.
- "Why trust it?"
  - Every scenario shows assumptions, ranges, and a concrete validation protocol.
